English slang words & phrases

British slang

Up for it – slang for being enthusiastic/willing to participate. For example: “I like bowling, I’m up for it tonight.”

Vibe – is slang for feelings, atmosphere, mood. For example, you may go to a club and say “I like the vibe in here the music is reem.”

Not my cup of tea – is a saying used when something is not to your liking. For example: “My boyfriend loves football but it’s not my cup of tea”.

Hammered – is the slang word used to describe someone who is very drunk. You can say someone is tipsy if they appear to be a bit drunk.

A cuppa - the shortened version of “a cup of tea.”

Cheeky - used to describe someone’s behavior. If someone is being “cheeky,” they are being slightly rude or disrespectful but in a charming or amusing way.

American slang

What’s up? – Hey; what are you doing?

I feel you – I understand and empathize with you. Eg. “I feel you. That was really unfair.”

I get it – I understand. Eg. “I get it now! Thank you for explaining that.”

My bad – My mistake. Eg. “My bad! I didn’t mean to do that.”

Sick – Awesome. Eg. “Those shoes are sick!”

It sucked – It was bad/poor quality. Eg. “That movie sucked.”

Australian slang

Accadacca – How Aussies refer to Australian band ACDC

Barbie – Barbecue

Servo – gas station

Bloody – very

She’ll be apples – it’ll be alright

Cactus – beaten, dead, finished, not working
